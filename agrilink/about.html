The Problem We Saw
India's Farmers Deserved Better
India has over 140 million farming families. Yet most of them have no idea who will buy their crop, at what price, or when they'll get paid — until it's too late.
Farmers receive only 20–30% of the final consumer price because of multiple middlemen taking commission at every step.
No price certainty before planting. Farmers grow crops without knowing if a buyer exists, leading to massive losses at harvest.
Industries struggle too. Food companies can't find reliable, quality-consistent farmers — leading to supply chain gaps and price volatility.
Northeast India is especially underserved. Farmers in Assam, Manipur and Meghalaya have incredible produce but almost no direct market access.
